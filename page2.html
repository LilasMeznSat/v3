ABOUT MEZNSAT

ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

The management structure of the project is shown below. The team structure consists of a project manager from the UAE Space Agency who overlooks the progress of the project. The principal investigator is Dr. Prashanth Marpu from Khalifa University for Science and Technology and the co-principal investigator is Dr. Abdul-Halim Jallad from the American University of Ras Al-Khaimah. 

The project organization consists of eight main teams that are responsible for one or more subsystems. Each team will be supervised by a faculty member and led by one student member. As MeznSat is a student project, the student teams on-board the project change almost every semester.

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

The project program is divided into five (5) distinct Work Packages (WP). These work packages may involve multiple institutions, with one institution responsible for overlooking the progress of that particular WP.

MISSION SCIENCE

Climate change has widely been attributed to the increase in greenhouse gases (GHG) in the atmosphere as a result of human activities. The impacts of climate change are expected to include shortage of water quantity and quality in most arid and semi-arid areas, and low agricultural productivity throughout the tropics and subtropics, accompanied by damage to ecosystems and biodiversity in these areas, and changes in forests and other ecosystems

Impact of Climate Change
1. Sea-Level Rise Coastal Flooding
2. Increased Salinity of Coastal Aquifers 
3.. Destruction of marine environment
4. Heat Stress
5. Frequent Extreme Weather Events (such as floods and droughts)
6. Increased Risk of Dust Storms
7. Risk from Airborne Xontaminants (e.g. Pesticides)

Main Science Objective â€“ GHG
The main science objective: the use of Short Wave Infrared (SWIR) to retrieve Carbon Dioxide and methane concentrations in the atmosphere.

Tentative Science Mission â€“ Red Tide

A tentative secondary mission: is to measure the total suspended matter (TSM) in the coastal waters of the UAE. TSM can be used as a proxy for nutrient concentration in the water.

The idea is to use the data to correlate with algae bloom occurrences and to develop a model to predict onset of algal blooms based on TSM measurements and sea surface temperatures observed by other satellites. 

SYSTEM

The satellite will be designed to be operated in low earth orbit for 3 years. The satellite should be designed to be compatible with most CubeSat deployer. The satellite weights about 4kg and measures 10cm x 10cm x 30cm.

GROUND SYSTEM

The ground station at Masdar Institute, Khalifa University of Science and Technology is designed to support multiple bands VHF, UHF, and S-band (receive only). The main goal of the MI ground station is to be able to send commands to the satellite, and receive data.

Ground Station components connection 

The GS PC is connected to two components (Transceivers and antenna controller). Each of these two components is connected to their power supply devices. The antenna controller is also connected to the antenna rotator which is connected to the antennas. The transceiver (Kenwood TS-2000) is connected to the preamplifiers through 30-meter feeder cable (LMR-000-UF). Each preamplifier is connected to an RF coax switch (CX2100N) by one-meter feeder cable. Then, these switches are connected to the duplexer (MX72N duplexer). Also, each switch is connected to a polarity switch through two a meter feeder cable and each switch is connected to a Yagi antenna. The duplexer (MX72N duplexer) is also connected to a multi-directional antenna (X700HNA Dual-band antenna) as a backup antenna. The SDR transceiver is connected to the S-band dish antenna (1.9-meter diameter).
